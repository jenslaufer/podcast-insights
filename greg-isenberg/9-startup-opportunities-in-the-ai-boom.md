# 9 Huge Startup Opportunities in the AI Boom

> Greg and Jonathan pressure-test 9 build-now niches across B2C, AI, mobile, and
> IRL. The meta-point: AI makes building cheap, so the edge moves to *distribution
> and audience* — pick a niche worth marrying, not a feature worth shipping.

## The three biggest bets (Greg's own ranking)

- **Biggest B2C: loneliness.** Third spaces, community apps, IRL hangouts.
- **Biggest B2B: managed AI employees** sold to small businesses.
- **Biggest overlooked: elder tech.** ~70M US boomers with money and unmet needs.

## The 9 opportunities

### 1. Live, unscripted creator shows (Twitch for business)
The polished interview podcast is losing to long, messy, live streams where the
host just talks. Proof point: **TBPN sold for >$100M** — low viewership but very
high-value viewers. As AI sanitizes the feed, **anti-AI authenticity** keeps
gaining share. Build the Twitch playbook for a professional audience.

### 2. Action apps (agent-first mobile)
Greg's favorite category. Today's apps assume a human taps, scrolls, types. The
**agent-first** version of an inbox, CRM, or expense tool *just does the work and
shows you only the exceptions*. This is the web→mobile-first transition repeating —
incumbents will lag the way Facebook lagged into Instagram. Rebuild a known tool
agent-native.

### 3. Loneliness, third spaces, niche communities
The biggest B2C bet. Examples: a **"Dads of Marathon" Discord — 13,900 members
for a single game**; Jonathan ran a **$90K painting retreat in February**. Why
*Central Perk* works and *Starbucks 2026* repels. The play: paid memberships,
niche Discords, and physical spaces that recreate the hangout. Study **222** and
**Fabric** as templates.

### 4. Elder tech for 65+
The biggest *overlooked* bet. **~70M US boomers**, disposable income, underserved
pain: hearing, mobility, social, memory, vision. Jonathan's own Facilitator.com
found its **highest-spending customers are 45+, reached via Facebook ads**. Build
for older adults — but don't make the landing page feel like a retirement home.

### 5. Adult hobbies
Adults pour money and identity into hobbies (the retreat above is one cut of
this). AI + community + commerce around a specific passion — instruction, gear,
events, peer groups. A niche worth marrying because the audience self-selects and
spends.

### 6. AI employees & AI agents
The biggest B2B bet. Recipe: **pick a vertical → pick a job title → list the 50
jobs-to-be-done → ship an agent that does them for ~1/10 of a junior's salary.**
Brand it as a **"junior," not a senior replacement** — that clears the trust bar
with small businesses.

### 7. Personalized nutrition/health by vertical
Jonathan lives with **GERD**, pays for blood-work services that give him nothing
actionable, and built his own **"Stomach Helper" Claude project** to make sense of
it. **~60M Americans have GERD.** The play: Zoe-style data + AI insights + meal
delivery, focused on **one condition** rather than generic wellness. Migraines,
IBS, etc. are equally open.

### 8. Pet health & AI for animals
Owners spend on pets like family but the tooling is thin. AI symptom triage,
health monitoring, and breed/condition-specific guidance — the personalized-health
playbook (#7) pointed at animals, where the emotional willingness-to-pay is high.

### 9. AI-native media companies done right
At the **top-1% quality bar**, not slop. **Rowan Chung-style AI avatars built 400K
followers in 18 months.** Slop loses; high-quality AI-assisted media wins —
*especially when paired with a product to sell*. Media becomes the distribution
arm of a real business.

## Stacking the ideas
The closing move: combine them — **live shows + retreats + a community of
entrepreneurs** — so audience, IRL events, and product reinforce each other
instead of competing for attention.

## Throughline
AI collapses the cost of building, so the moat shifts to **audience, trust, and
distribution**. Every winning idea here pairs an underserved niche with a channel
the founder can actually own (Discord, Facebook ads, live streams, owned media).
