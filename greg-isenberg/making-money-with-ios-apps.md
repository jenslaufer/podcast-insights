# Making $$$ with iOS apps

> George, 19, walks Greg through the exact playbook he used to take AI-built
> mobile apps to $10K/month — and his hit **WrestleAI** to **100K+ downloads and
> ~$200K revenue**. The thesis: in 2026 anyone can *build* an app, so the moat is
> no longer code — it's **a single marketable "gotcha" feature + distribution**.
> Marketing is the product. (48-min guest episode.)

## The 5-step app playbook

1. **Start from an idea you actually care about.** Reverse-engineer a concept
   that's already going viral in a niche you'd happily live inside for a year.
2. **Design the UI/UX first.** In a market where building is free, look is the
   differentiator — the app has to *feel* premium before it does anything.
3. **Ship one standout "gotcha feature."** The single thing that makes the app
   screenshot-able and explainable in one line. Everything else is table stakes.
4. **Build conversion-focused onboarding.** The onboarding *is* the sales page —
   its only job is to get to the paywall with intent. Optimize it like a funnel,
   not like a tutorial.
5. **Distribute through influencers + paid ads.** Don't wait for organic; buy
   attention deliberately (numbers below).

## Marketing tips (the core of the episode)

### Make the feature marketable, not just useful
- The **"gotcha feature"** exists for *marketability*, not utility — pick the one
  feature you can show in a 3-second clip and that makes a stranger say "wait,
  how?" If you can't make the demo clip, you don't have the feature yet.
- Reverse-engineer **what's already going viral** and rebuild it cleaner for a
  specific niche, instead of inventing demand from scratch.

### Instagram as the funnel (organic social proof)
- **Instagram is the sales funnel** and the social-proof engine — reels of the
  gotcha feature drive both discovery and trust. Treat the IG account as the
  storefront, not the app store page.
- Social proof (download counts, reactions, creator endorsements) is what
  converts cold traffic — manufacture it visibly.

### Influencer outreach — buy cheap CPMs
- **Target ~$2 CPM influencer deals.** Go wide on small/mid creators in the niche
  rather than one expensive name; you're buying impressions cheaply, not prestige.
- Influencer clips double as ad creative (see paid below) and as the IG social
  proof — one shoot, three uses.

### Paid advertising fundamentals
- **Launch 5–15 creatives** per test — you don't know which hook wins, so let the
  ad platform find it.
- **Budget ~$100/day** to test; kill losers fast, scale the one that beats your
  ARPU math.
- Creative volume > creative polish: more shots on goal beats one "perfect" ad.

### The metrics that decide everything
- **$2 ARPU target** (average revenue per user). Every acquisition decision is:
  *can I buy a user for less than ~$2 and keep them long enough to clear it?*
- Watch **conversion rate** (install → paywall → pay) and **retention** — a
  great gotcha feature with bad retention just means you're renting users.
- The model only works when **paid-acquisition cost < ARPU × retention**. If the
  ad math doesn't close, the app doesn't scale no matter how good it looks.

## Why this matters for us

- **Distribution-first, not feature-first** — same lesson as Greg's "9
  opportunities" note: building is cheap, the edge is the gotcha + the funnel.
  Maps directly to the launch-kit *idea-validation* thesis (test the offer×audience
  fast, the plumbing was never the bottleneck — cf. ExtCharge 0/593).
- The **$2 CPM / $100-day / 5–15 creatives / $2 ARPU** numbers are a concrete,
  copyable paid-ads starting recipe if we ever push a consumer app or a FK/HC
  tool with paid acquisition.
- "Onboarding *is* the sales page" is a reusable conversion frame for any
  lead-capture funnel.
